\section{Introduction}

Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) remains one of the most prevalent infectious diseases in tropical and subtropical regions worldwide. This disease is caused by the dengue virus and is primarily transmitted through the bite of infected \textit{Aedes aegypti} mosquitoes. According to the World Health Organization (WHO), dengue cases have increased significantly over the past two decades, with an estimated 100--400 million infections occurring annually, particularly in Southeast Asia and Latin America \cite{b1}. The high incidence rate poses major challenges to healthcare systems, especially in developing countries with limited medical resources \cite{b2}. Therefore, timely and accurate diagnosis is essential to reduce the risk of complications and mortality associated with DHF \cite{b3}.

Dengue diagnosis is generally performed through clinical symptom evaluation and laboratory examinations, including platelet count, hematocrit, leukocyte count, and hemoglobin levels \cite{b4}. However, the diagnostic process often faces several challenges, particularly when dealing with large patient volumes or symptoms that overlap with other infectious diseases. In addition, manual interpretation of medical data may lead to inconsistencies in clinical decision-making. In this context, advancements in \textit{Artificial Intelligence} (AI), particularly \textit{Machine Learning} (ML) and \textit{Deep Learning} (DL), offer promising approaches due to their capability to automatically identify complex relationships within medical data \cite{b5}. Previous studies have also demonstrated that DL-based approaches achieve strong performance in disease prediction and medical data classification involving heterogeneous characteristics \cite{b6, b7}.

Nevertheless, studies investigating the impact of preprocessing techniques, particularly data normalization, on the performance of Deep Learning models for dengue classification remain relatively limited. Variations in feature scales within healthcare datasets may influence training stability and prediction performance \cite{b8}. Furthermore, appropriate evaluation methods, such as \textit{Cross Validation}, are required to ensure better generalization capability and reduce the risk of overfitting \cite{b9}.

Based on these considerations, this study develops a Deep Learning-based DHF classification model utilizing clinical symptoms and laboratory examination data. The primary focus of this research is to compare the effects of normalization techniques and \textit{Cross Validation} scenarios on model performance. Experiments were conducted using two dataset types, namely the \textit{full-feature dataset} and the \textit{symptom-only dataset}, with three normalization approaches: no normalization, \textit{StandardScaler}, and \textit{MinMaxScaler}. Model evaluation was performed using \textit{K-Fold Cross Validation} to obtain more stable and reliable results. This study is expected to contribute to the development of more effective and accurate intelligent medical decision support systems.

\subsection{Research Contributions}

The main contributions of this study are summarized as follows:

\begin{enumerate}
    \item This study develops a Deep Learning-based classification model for Dengue Hemorrhagic Fever (DHF) by utilizing clinical symptom data and laboratory examination results to support faster and more accurate diagnosis.

    \item This study compares two dataset scenarios, namely the f\textit{full-feature dataset} and the \textit{symptom-only dataset}, while also evaluating several normalization techniques, including no normalization, \textit{StandardScaler}, and \textit{MinMaxScaler}, to examine their impact on classification performance.

    \item To ensure more reliable results, the proposed model is evaluated using \textit{K-Fold Cross Validation}, providing a better understanding of model stability and the influence of preprocessing strategies on Deep Learning-based dengue classification.
\end{enumerate}

\section{Related Work}

Previous studies have demonstrated that Machine Learning and Deep Learning approaches are effective for Dengue Fever classification and prediction. Andrade Girón \textit{et al}.~\cite{b10} conducted a systematic review showing that ANN, CNN, Random Forest, SVM, and XGBoost are commonly applied for dengue diagnosis. Their review also reported that normalization methods such as MinMaxScaler and Z-score normalization, combined with 10-Fold and Stratified K-Fold Cross Validation, improved model performance and generalization.

Several researchers specifically emphasized the importance of normalization and Cross Validation in healthcare classification systems. Ravi \textit{et al}.~\cite{b11} explained that Z-score normalization improves Deep Learning performance on biomedical datasets, while 5-Fold and 10-Fold Cross Validation help reduce overfitting. Similarly, Behera \textit{et al}.~\cite{b16} showed that StandardScaler normalization and 10-Fold Cross Validation increased classification stability in biomedical data analysis. Cholet~\cite{b18} also highlighted that normalization techniques stabilize Deep Learning training and improve convergence.

In the context of Dengue Fever classification, Hamdani \textit{et al}.~\cite{b12} applied Support Vector Machine (SVM) with standardized clinical data and achieved 99.1\% accuracy using 5-Fold and 10-Fold Cross Validation. Another study by Hamdani \textit{et al}.~\cite{b14} utilized Artificial Neural Networks for dengue diagnosis using symptom data and obtained 97.17\% accuracy after applying feature normalization and K-Fold validation.

Deep Learning-based approaches have also shown promising results for dengue prediction. Ramasamy \textit{et al}.~\cite{b13,b17} proposed a Neural Network model with feature optimization and StandardScaler normalization, achieving 97.1\% accuracy using 5-Fold Cross Validation. Anibal \textit{et al}.~\cite{b15} and Majeed \textit{et al}.~\cite{b20} implemented Deep Learning models for dengue forecasting using MinMaxScaler normalization and validation techniques to improve prediction reliability.

Other studies compared multiple Machine Learning algorithms using clinical and laboratory datasets. Abdualgalil \textit{et al}.~\cite{b21} applied Z-score normalization, SMOTE+ENN balancing, and 10-Fold Cross Validation, where the Extra Tree Classifier achieved 99.03\% accuracy. Sriyanto \textit{et al}.~\cite{b22} compared several Machine Learning algorithms using StandardScaler normalization and reported that Neural Network and Decision Tree models achieved the highest accuracy of 99.4\%.

In Indonesia, Dewi \textit{et al}.~\cite{b23} developed a Machine Learning model for predicting dengue cases and severity using Min-Max scaling and reported that XGBoost achieved the best performance with 85\% accuracy. Xu and Xiao~\cite{b19} also emphasized that feature scaling and Cross Validation improve model generalization in dengue prediction systems.

Based on previous studies, normalization techniques and Cross Validation significantly influence the performance and stability of healthcare classification models. However, limited studies comprehensively compare multiple normalization methods and dataset scenarios for Deep Learning-based Dengue Fever classification. Therefore, this study focuses on comparative analysis of normalization techniques and Cross Validation scenarios using both full clinical-laboratory datasets and symptom-only datasets.

\subsection{Data Preprocessing}

Data preprocessing was performed to improve dataset quality before the training process. This stage is important to ensure that the input data can be processed effectively by the Deep Learning model. The preprocessing stage in this study consists of several steps, including data cleaning, handling missing values, feature encoding, and data normalization.

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Data Cleaning}

The data cleaning process was conducted to identify inconsistent, duplicated, or invalid data within the dataset. Several records containing incomplete or irrelevant information were examined to maintain data quality and improve model performance.

\item \textbf{Missing Value Handling}

Missing values in the dataset were handled using appropriate preprocessing techniques. Numerical attributes with missing values were replaced using statistical approaches to maintain dataset consistency and avoid information loss during model training.

\item \textbf{Feature Encoding}

Categorical attributes such as gender and diagnosis labels were transformed into numerical representations using encoding techniques. This process allows the Deep Learning model to process categorical data effectively during the classification stage.

\item \textbf{Data Normalization}

Feature normalization was applied to reduce differences in numerical scale among attributes. In this study, three normalization approaches were evaluated, namely without normalization, \textit{StandardScaler}, and \textit{MinMaxScaler}. The normalization process aims to improve training stability and optimize classification performance.

\end{itemize}

% =====================================================
% NORMALIZATION EXPERIMENTS
% =====================================================

Normalization experiments were conducted to analyze the effect of feature scaling on the performance of the Deep Learning classification model. Since the dataset contains attributes with different numerical ranges, normalization is important to ensure that all features contribute proportionally during the training process.

In this study, three normalization approaches were evaluated:

\begin{itemize}

\item \textbf{Without Normalization}

The original dataset was directly used without applying any scaling technique. This experiment was conducted to observe baseline model performance.

\item \textbf{StandardScaler}

StandardScaler transforms the data distribution into a standard normal distribution with a mean of 0 and standard deviation of 1. This method is commonly used to stabilize the learning process in Deep Learning models.

\item \textbf{MinMaxScaler}

MinMaxScaler transforms feature values into a fixed range between 0 and 1. This method is widely used in Artificial Neural Networks because it helps improve gradient optimization and training convergence.

\end{itemize}

Each normalization method was applied to two dataset configurations, namely the complete dataset containing clinical symptoms and laboratory features, and the symptoms-only dataset. The experimental results from all normalization methods were then compared based on classification performance metrics.
